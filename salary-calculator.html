The Salary Conversion Formula

The salary conversion formula is straightforward:

Hourly Rate = Annual Salary / (Hours per Week × Weeks per Year)
Daily Rate = Hourly Rate × 8
Weekly Pay = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week
Bi‑Weekly Pay = Weekly Pay × 2
Monthly Pay = Annual Salary / 12

Daily Rate always assumes a standard 8‑hour workday for consistency across comparisons, even if you set a different number of hours per week. If you work, say, 35 hours over 5 days (7 hrs/day), your actual daily earnings will differ slightly from this figure.

Here's how the formulas apply to a $75,000 annual salary at 40 hours/week and 52 weeks/year:

  • Hourly Rate: $75,000 / (40 × 52) = $36.06
  • Daily Rate: $36.06 × 8 = $288.46
  • Weekly Pay: $36.06 × 40 = $1,442.31
  • Bi‑Weekly Pay: $1,442.31 × 2 = $2,884.62
  • Monthly Pay: $75,000 / 12 = $6,250

Gross Pay vs Net Pay — What's the Difference?

TermDescription
Gross Pay Your total earnings before taxes and deductions.
Net Pay Your take-home pay after taxes and deductions — what you actually receive.

This salary calculator shows both gross and net pay, so you can see the full picture of your earnings.

How Overtime, Part-Time, and Irregular Hours Affect These Numbers

  • Overtime pay – this calculator assumes a fixed hourly rate. If your overtime hours are paid at 1.5x or 2x, calculate your overtime hours separately and add them to your base monthly pay.
  • Part-time work – lower your "hours per week" input to get an accurate hourly and weekly figure; monthly and annual totals adjust automatically.
  • Unpaid time off – if you take unpaid leave, your actual annual take-home will be lower than this calculator shows, since it assumes pay for all 52 weeks (or your chosen week count).
  • Variable schedules – if your hours change week to week, use your average weekly hours over a few months for a more realistic estimate.

Frequently Asked Questions About Salary Conversion

1. How do I convert annual salary to hourly rate?
Divide your annual salary by your total working hours for the year: Hourly Rate = Annual Salary / (Hours per Week × Weeks per Year). For example, $75,000 / (40 × 52) = $36.06 per hour.
2. What is the difference between gross pay and net pay?
Gross pay is your total earnings before taxes and deductions. Net pay is what actually lands in your bank account after taxes are taken out.
3. How many working weeks are in a year?
A standard full-time year is 52 weeks (2,080 hours at 40 hours/week). If you're paid through holidays and PTO, you can keep this at 52 — if not, lower the figure to match your actual paid weeks.
4. How do I calculate my monthly salary from annual salary?
Divide your annual salary by 12. For example, $75,000 / 12 = $6,250 per month before taxes.
5. What is a good hourly rate?
It depends heavily on your industry, location, and experience level — there's no single benchmark. Converting your salary or target pay to an hourly figure makes it easier to compare against typical rates in your field and region.
6. Should I negotiate salary based on annual or hourly rate?
For salaried roles, negotiate on annual salary since that's how the offer is structured. For contract or freelance work, negotiate on hourly rate so you can compare offers with different time commitments on equal footing.
7. How accurate is this salary calculator?
The pay-period conversions (hourly, daily, weekly, monthly) are exact, based on standard formulas. The after-tax figure is a simplified estimate using the flat tax rate you enter — actual tax owed depends on your country's brackets, deductions, and credits, so treat it as a planning estimate rather than a final number.
