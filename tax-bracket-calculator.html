Marginal Tax Rate vs Effective Tax Rate — What's the Difference?

MetricDescriptionExample
Marginal Tax Rate The rate you pay on your next dollar of income — your highest tax bracket If you're in the 24% bracket, your marginal rate is 24%
Effective Tax Rate The average rate you pay on all your income Total tax ÷ Total income × 100

For example, if your income is $75,000 and you pay $10,500 in taxes:

  • Effective Tax Rate: 14% ($10,500 ÷ $75,000)
  • Marginal Tax Rate: 22% (your highest bracket)

This progressive tax calculator shows you both rates instantly.

How Progressive Tax Brackets Work

A progressive tax system means different portions of your income are taxed at different rates. You don't pay a single rate on your entire income — instead, each bracket of income is taxed at its corresponding rate.

For example, with a simple three‑bracket system:

  • 10% on income up to $10,000
  • 15% on income from $10,001 to $40,000
  • 25% on income over $40,000

If you earn $50,000:

  • The first $10,000 is taxed at 10% = $1,000
  • The next $30,000 is taxed at 15% = $4,500
  • The remaining $10,000 is taxed at 25% = $2,500
  • Total Tax: $8,000
  • Effective Rate: 16%
  • Marginal Rate: 25%
