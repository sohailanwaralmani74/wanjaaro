Markup vs Margin – What's the Difference?

Understanding the difference between markup and margin is crucial for business owners:

  • Markup – calculated as a percentage of cost. It tells you how much you're adding to the cost to determine the selling price. This is used for pricing decisions.
  • Margin – calculated as a percentage of revenue. It tells you what percentage of each dollar of revenue is profit. This is used for profitability analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions About Markup

1. What is markup and how is it calculated?
Markup is the percentage added to the cost price to determine the selling price. The markup formula is: Markup = (Selling Price – Cost) / Cost × 100. For example, if a product costs $50 and sells for $75, the markup is 50%.
2. What is the difference between markup and margin?
Markup is calculated as a percentage of cost, while margin is calculated as a percentage of revenue. For example, a 50% markup equals a 33.3% margin. Markup is used for pricing decisions, while margin measures overall profitability.
3. How do I find the selling price from a target markup?
Use the "Price from Cost & Markup" mode in this markup calculator online. Enter your cost and desired markup percentage, and the tool calculates the selling price using: Selling Price = Cost × (1 + Target Markup / 100).
4. What is a good markup percentage for my business?
The ideal markup percentage varies by industry. Retail often uses 50-100% markup, while wholesale may use 10-30%. Grocery stores typically use low markups (10-20%), while luxury goods can have 200%+ markups. Use this markup calculator to test different scenarios.
5. What is cost-plus pricing?
Cost-plus pricing is a strategy where you add a fixed markup percentage to your cost to determine the selling price. This selling price calculator helps you implement cost-plus pricing by showing the exact selling price for any markup percentage.
6. Can I calculate markup online for free?
Yes! You can calculate markup completely free with this markup calculator online. No registration, download, or payment is required – just open the page, enter your numbers, and get instant results. This markup percentage calculator is available online 24/7.
7. How does markup affect profit margins?
Higher markup percentages generally lead to higher profit margins, but they can also reduce sales volume if prices become too high. This markup calculator helps you find the right balance by showing both markup and its equivalent margin percentage.
