Gross Profit Margin vs Net Margin – What's the Difference?

Understanding the difference between gross profit margin and net margin is crucial for business owners:

  • Gross Profit Margin – measures the percentage of revenue remaining after deducting only the cost of goods sold (COGS). It shows how efficiently you produce or source your products.
  • Net Margin – takes into account all expenses, including taxes, interest, overhead, and operating costs. It shows your overall business profitability.

This gross profit margin calculator focuses on gross margin by default, but you can use the results as a foundation to calculate net margin by factoring in your additional expenses separately.

Contribution Margin Calculator – A Quick Note

While this tool focuses on gross profit margin, it's worth understanding the contribution margin calculator concept. Contribution margin is calculated as:

Contribution Margin = Revenue – Variable Costs

Unlike gross margin (which includes all costs), contribution margin only considers variable costs. This helps businesses determine how much revenue is available to cover fixed costs and generate profit. You can use this margin calculator as a foundation to calculate contribution margin by entering variable costs instead of total costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Profit Margin

1. What is profit margin and how is it calculated?
Profit margin is the percentage of revenue that remains after deducting costs. The profit margin formula is: Profit Margin = (Revenue – Cost) / Revenue × 100. For example, if you sell a product for $100 and it costs $70, your profit margin is 30%.
2. What is the difference between profit margin and markup?
Profit margin is calculated as a percentage of revenue, while markup is calculated as a percentage of cost. Markup = (Revenue – Cost) / Cost × 100. For example, a 25% profit margin equals a 33.3% markup. This gross profit calculator shows both values so you can see the relationship.
3. How do I find the selling price from a target margin?
Use the "Price from Cost & Margin" mode in this profit margin calculator. Enter your cost and desired margin percentage, and the tool calculates the required selling price. The formula is: Revenue = Cost / (1 – Target Margin / 100).
4. What is a good profit margin for my business?
The ideal gross profit margin varies by industry. Generally, 10% is average, 20% is good, and 30%+ is excellent. Retail tends to have lower margins (2-10%), while software and services can have much higher margins (50-90%). Use this gross profit margin calculator to benchmark your business against industry standards.
5. What is gross margin vs net margin?
Gross profit margin is profit after deducting only the cost of goods sold (COGS). Net margin considers all expenses, including taxes, interest, and overhead. This gross profit calculator focuses on gross margin, which you can then use as a foundation to calculate net margin by factoring in your additional expenses.
6. What is a contribution margin calculator?
A contribution margin calculator measures the percentage of revenue that remains after deducting only variable costs. It differs from gross profit margin, which includes all costs. You can use this margin calculator as a contribution margin calculator by entering variable costs instead of total costs.
