The Credit Card Payoff Formula

The credit card payoff formula calculates how long it takes to eliminate your balance based on your monthly payment and interest rate:

n = −log(1 − (r × P) / M) / log(1 + r) Where: n = number of months, r = monthly interest rate, P = principal (balance), M = monthly payment

For example, if you owe $5,000 at 22% interest and pay $200 per month, you'll be debt free in approximately 34 months — and you'll pay $2,076 in total interest.

What Is a Credit Card Amortization Schedule?

A credit card amortization schedule is a complete breakdown of every payment over the life of your credit card debt. It shows how much of each payment goes toward principal and interest, and what your remaining balance is after each payment.

Early in your repayment, a larger portion of each payment goes toward interest. Over time, as the principal decreases, more of your payment goes toward the principal. This credit card amortization schedule is generated instantly by this credit card repayment calculator.

How to Pay Off Your Credit Card Faster

  • Pay more than the minimum – increasing your monthly payment reduces interest and shortens your payoff timeline. Use this credit card payment calculator to see the impact.
  • Make bi‑weekly payments – paying half your monthly payment every two weeks can reduce interest and accelerate payoff.
  • Consider a balance transfer – transferring to a 0% APR card can save you thousands in interest.
  • Avoid new charges – stop using the card while paying it off to prevent the balance from growing.

Frequently Asked Questions About Credit Card Payoff

1. How long does it take to pay off a credit card?
The time it takes depends on your balance, interest rate, and monthly payment. This credit card payoff calculator shows you exactly how long it will take with your specific numbers.
2. How is credit card interest calculated?
Credit card interest is calculated daily using your annual percentage rate (APR). This credit card payment calculator uses monthly compounding, which is the standard for most payoff calculations.
3. What is a good monthly payment for paying off credit card debt?
A "good" monthly payment is one that exceeds your minimum payment and fits your budget. The higher your payment, the faster you'll become debt free and the less interest you'll pay. Use this credit card debt repayment calculator to find the right balance.
4. What is an amortization schedule?
A credit card amortization schedule is a complete breakdown of every monthly payment over the life of your debt, showing principal, interest, and remaining balance. This credit card payoff calculator generates it instantly.
5. Should I pay off my credit card or save money first?
Generally, pay off high‑interest credit card debt (above 10%) as quickly as possible. The interest on credit cards is typically much higher than what you'd earn in a savings account. Use this credit card debt payoff calculator to see the true cost of your debt.
6. Can I use this calculator for multiple credit cards?
This calculator is designed for a single credit card. For multiple cards, consider using our debt avalanche vs snowball calculator to compare payoff strategies across multiple debts.
7. How accurate is this credit card payoff calculator?
This credit card calculator pay off uses standard financial formulas and is accurate for planning. However, actual results may vary if your interest rate changes or if you miss payments. Always check with your card issuer for final numbers.
