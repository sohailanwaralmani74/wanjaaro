What Is a Dividend Yield Calculator?

A dividend yield calculator computes the annual dividend income you can expect from a stock or exchange‑traded fund (ETF) relative to its current share price. The dividend yield formula is: Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividend Per Share) / (Share Price), expressed as a percentage. For example, if a stock pays $4 per year in dividends and trades at $100, its yield is 4%.

Our stock dividend yield calculator goes beyond the basic formula. You can input monthly or quarterly dividends, add a projected growth rate, compare yields across multiple stocks, and even estimate the total return when dividends are reinvested. The tool also provides a year‑by‑year projection if you plan to hold and reinvest.

Frequently Asked Questions About Dividend Yield Calculators

1. What is dividend yield?
Dividend yield is a financial ratio that shows how much a company pays out in dividends each year relative to its share price. It is expressed as a percentage.
2. What is the dividend yield formula?
The dividend yield equation is: Annual Dividend Per Share ÷ Share Price. For example, if a stock pays $2 per share annually and trades at $40, the yield is 5%.
3. What is a good dividend yield?
A “good” yield varies by sector. Utilities and REITs often yield 4-8%, while tech companies yield lower (<2%). A very high yield (over 10%) may be unsustainable.
4. How do you calculate dividend yield for a portfolio?
For a portfolio, sum the annual dividend income from all holdings, then divide by the total portfolio value. Our calculator can handle individual stocks; for a portfolio you may run separate calculations.
5. Does the calculator support monthly or quarterly dividends?
Yes – if you know the quarterly or monthly amount, enter the annualised figure (e.g., $0.50 per quarter × 4 = $2 annual dividend). The yield formula uses the annual dividend.
6. How do I find the annual dividend per share for a stock?
Look for “dividend per share” on financial websites (Yahoo Finance, Morningstar) or in a company’s investor relations page. Use the trailing twelve months (TTM) figure.
7. What is the difference between dividend yield and dividend return?
Dividend yield is the annual dividend divided by share price. Dividend return (total return) includes both dividends and share price appreciation. Our calculator focuses on yield, with an optional reinvestment projection for total return.
8. Can I calculate the dividend yield of an ETF?
Absolutely. Enter the ETF’s share price and its 12‑month dividend distribution per share. The stock yield calculator works identically for ETFs.
9. How do I compute dividend yield if I know the stock’s yield from last year?
Yield changes with share price. If you know last year’s dividend per share and today’s price, use the formula with the current price. Don’t rely on historical yield percentages.
10. What is a dividend growth calculator?
A dividend growth calculator projects future dividend income assuming the company raises its dividend by a fixed percentage each year. Our calculator can include a growth rate for forward projections.
11. Does reinvesting dividends make a difference?
Yes – reinvesting dividends buys more shares, which in turn generate more dividends. This compounding effect can dramatically increase long‑term total returns. Our tool has a “reinvest” toggle to show this effect.
12. What is the dividend yield ratio formula?
The dividend yield ratio is the same as the basic formula: annual dividend per share divided by share price. It is often used in value investing to compare income potential.
13. Can I use this calculator for international stocks?
Yes – select the appropriate currency from the dropdown (USD, EUR, GBP, INR, etc.). The dividend yield calculation is currency‑agnostic as long as both price and dividend are in the same currency.
14. How do I find the dividend yield of a preferred stock?
Preferred stocks pay fixed dividends. Enter the annual preferred dividend amount and the preferred share price. The formula is the same.
15. Is a higher dividend yield always better?
Not necessarily. An extremely high yield may indicate a falling stock price or a dividend that may be cut. Always check the payout ratio and the company’s financial health.
16. What is the dividend per share formula?
Dividend per share = Total dividends paid / Number of outstanding shares. Our calculator asks for dividend per share directly, which you can obtain from financial statements.
17. Can I calculate dividend yield on cost (YOC)?
Yield on cost is the annual dividend divided by the original purchase price, not the current price. Our calculator focuses on current yield, but you can manually compute YOC using the same formula with your cost basis.
18. How does stock price volatility affect dividend yield?
When a stock price falls, the dividend yield rises (even if the dividend is unchanged). This can create a false signal of value if the price drop is due to underlying problems. Use yield as one of several metrics.
19. Is this dividend yield calculator free?
Yes – completely free, no signup, no ads. Use it unlimited times to evaluate stocks and ETFs.

Yes – click the “Share” button to copy a URL with your inputs. You can also download the projection as a CSV file if reinvestment is enabled.
