The Hourly to Salary Formula

Converting hourly pay to salary is straightforward:

Annual Salary = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week × Weeks per Year
Monthly Salary = Annual Salary ÷ 12
Weekly Salary = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week
Bi‑Weekly Salary = Weekly Salary × 2
Daily Salary = Hourly Rate × 8

For example, if your hourly rate is $25, working 40 hours per week for 52 weeks:

  • Annual Salary: $25 × 40 × 52 = $52,000
  • Monthly Salary: $52,000 ÷ 12 = $4,333
  • Weekly Salary: $25 × 40 = $1,000

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I calculate annual salary from hourly rate?
Multiply your hourly rate by hours per week and weeks per year. For example, $25 × 40 × 52 = $52,000. This hourly to salary calculator does the math instantly.
2. How many hours are in a typical work year?
A typical full‑time work year has 2,080 hours (40 hours × 52 weeks). This hourly wage calculator lets you adjust both hours and weeks.
3. What is a good hourly rate?
A "good" hourly rate depends on your industry, experience, and location. Use this hourly income calculator to see how your rate translates to annual salary.
4. How do I convert salary to hourly rate?
Divide your annual salary by hours per week and weeks per year. For example, $52,000 / (40 × 52) = $25/hour.
5. Can I use this for freelance or contract work?
Yes. This hourly pay calculator is perfect for freelancers and contractors. Enter your hourly rate and see your projected annual, monthly, and weekly income.
6. How does overtime affect my salary?
Overtime is typically paid at 1.5× your regular hourly rate. This tool uses standard hours — for overtime calculations, add your overtime hours separately.
7. How accurate is this hourly to salary calculator?
This annual salary from hourly calculator uses standard formulas and is accurate for planning. Actual earnings may vary based on overtime, bonuses, and deductions.
