How Progressive Tax Brackets Work

A progressive tax system means different portions of your income are taxed at different rates. You don't pay a single rate on your entire income — instead, each bracket of income is taxed at its corresponding rate.

For example, under the 2024 US federal tax brackets for married filing jointly:

  • 10% on income up to $23,200
  • 12% on income from $23,201 to $94,300
  • 22% on income from $94,301 to $201,050
  • 24% on income from $201,051 to $383,900
  • 32% on income from $383,901 to $487,450
  • 35% on income from $487,451 to $731,200
  • 37% on income over $731,200

This tax calculator automatically applies the correct brackets for your filing status and tax year.

Effective Tax Rate vs Marginal Tax Rate — What's the Difference?

MetricDescriptionExample
Effective Tax Rate The average rate you pay on your total income Total tax ÷ Total income × 100
Marginal Tax Rate The rate you pay on your next dollar of income Your highest tax bracket

For example, if your income is $100,000 and you pay $15,000 in taxes:

  • Effective Tax Rate: 15% ($15,000 ÷ $100,000)
  • Marginal Tax Rate: 22% (your highest bracket)

Frequently Asked Questions About Income Tax

1. How do I calculate my income tax?
Use a taxable income calculator like this one. Enter your income, filing status, and tax year. The calculator for tax applies the correct brackets and shows your total tax liability, effective rate, and marginal rate.
2. What is the difference between effective and marginal tax rate?
Your effective tax rate is the average rate you pay on all your income. Your marginal tax rate is the rate you'd pay on your next dollar of income. This income tax calculator shows both.
3. What is a tax bracket?
A tax bracket is a range of income taxed at a specific rate. In a progressive tax system, different portions of your income are taxed at different rates. This tax bracket calculator shows you exactly which brackets apply to you.
4. How does the standard deduction affect my taxes?
The standard deduction reduces your taxable income. This federal income tax calculator automatically applies the correct standard deduction based on your filing status and tax year.
5. Can I use this calculator for state taxes?
This income tax estimator focuses on federal income tax. State tax rates vary widely — check with your state's tax authority for state‑specific calculations.
6. What is my filing status?
Your filing status determines which tax brackets and deductions apply to you. Common statuses include single, married filing jointly, and head of household. Select the correct status in this tax liability calculator for accurate results.
7. How accurate is this income tax calculator?
This online tax calculator uses standard tax brackets and formulas. However, it's for estimation purposes only. Actual tax liability may vary based on deductions, credits, and other factors. Consult a tax professional for definitive advice.
