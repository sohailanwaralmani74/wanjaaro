What Is a SIP Return Calculator?

A SIP return calculator (Systematic Investment Plan calculator) estimates the future value of regular monthly investments in mutual funds, stocks, or recurring deposits. Unlike a lump sum calculator, a systematic investment plan calculator accounts for periodic contributions, allowing you to see how disciplined monthly savings grow with compounding. Our monthly SIP calculator also supports step‑up (annual increase), inflation adjustment, and daily, monthly, or yearly compounding.

Whether you need a SIP calculator with inflation for retirement planning or a lump sum SIP calculator to compare one‑time vs. periodic investments, this tool gives you a year‑by‑year breakdown, interactive charts, and the ability to download your data as CSV.

How to Use This Systematic Investment Plan Calculator

1 Enter your monthly SIP amount – the fixed sum you invest each month.
2 Set the expected annual return rate – e.g., 12% for equity mutual funds, 7% for debt funds.
3 Choose investment duration – in years or months (our calculator supports both).
4 Add optional step‑up percentage – increase your SIP annually to match income growth.
5 Enable inflation adjustment – see your real returns in today’s purchasing power.
6 Choose compounding frequency – monthly (standard for SIPs), daily, quarterly, or annually.
7 Click Calculate – view the final corpus, total invested, interest earned, and year‑by‑year growth chart.

Frequently Asked Questions About SIP Return Calculators

1. What is a SIP return calculator?
A SIP return calculator estimates the future value of regular monthly investments under compound interest. It shows total invested, interest earned, and final corpus based on expected return rate and duration.
2. How does a systematic investment plan calculator work?
It uses the SIP formula: future value = monthly amount × [{(1 + r)^n – 1} / r] × (1 + r), where r is monthly rate and n is number of months. It accounts for each monthly contribution compounding separately.
3. What is the difference between a SIP calculator and a lump sum calculator?
A SIP calculator assumes periodic (usually monthly) investments, while a lump sum calculator assumes a single one‑time investment. Our lump sum SIP calculator lets you compare both strategies side‑by‑side.
4. Can I calculate SIP returns with step‑up (annual increase)?
Yes – this SIP investment calculator includes an optional step‑up percentage. For example, increase your monthly SIP by 10% every year to match salary growth.
5. Does this SIP calculator account for inflation?
Yes – toggle the SIP calculator with inflation feature to see your real returns in today’s purchasing power. Enter an expected inflation rate (e.g., 6%).
6. What is a realistic return rate for mutual fund SIPs?
Equity mutual funds historically give 10–14% over long periods (10+ years). Debt funds give 6–8%. Use conservative estimates like 10% for planning.
7. Can I use this for monthly SIP in Indian Rupees (INR)?
Absolutely. The calculator supports multiple currencies – select INR from the currency dropdown. It works as a mutual fund SIP calculator in INR.
8. What is the minimum SIP amount I can enter?
You can enter any positive monthly amount – from ₹500 up to any value. Many mutual funds start at ₹500, but this calculator has no lower bound.
9. How does compounding frequency affect SIP returns?
Monthly compounding is standard for SIPs. Daily gives slightly higher returns, annual gives lower. The difference is small – less than 0.5% over 10 years.
10. Can I calculate SIP for a duration in months (not years)?
Yes – this SIP calculator months option lets you enter duration in months for short‑term goals (e.g., 24 months for a down payment).
11. What is the difference between SIP return rate and CAGR?
SIP return rate (XIRR) accounts for multiple cash flows over time. CAGR assumes a single lump sum. Our calculator computes the effective annualised return similar to XIRR.
12. Can I compare SIP vs. lump sum with the same calculator?
Yes – our SIP and lumpsum calculator allows you to enter both a monthly SIP amount and an optional one‑time lump sum at the start to compare outcomes.
13. How do I download my SIP calculation data?
Click the “Download CSV” button below the chart. The CSV includes year‑by‑year invested amount, corpus, interest earned, and annual returns.
14. Is this calculator free for unlimited use?
Yes – 100% free, no signup, no hidden charges. Use it as a mutual fund SIP planner as many times as you need.
15. Can I use this for recurring deposits (RD) as well?
Absolutely – a recurring deposit is mathematically identical to a SIP. Just set the interest rate to your bank’s RD rate (e.g., 7%) and monthly investment amount.
16. What is the maximum investment period allowed?
Up to 50 years. The slider and input cap at 50 years, which covers most retirement and long‑term goal planning.
17. Does the calculator show pre‑tax or post‑tax returns?
It shows pre‑tax returns. For post‑tax estimates, reduce the expected return rate manually – e.g., 12% pre‑tax becomes ~10.2% after 15% LTCG tax.
18. Can I calculate SIP returns for daily, weekly, or quarterly intervals?
Yes – our daily SIP calculator and quarterly options are available via the compounding frequency dropdown. Choose daily for micro‑saving apps, quarterly for some dividend plans.
19. What is the formula used for SIP calculation?
We use the standard formula: FV = P × [((1 + r)^n – 1) / r] × (1 + r), where P = monthly investment, r = monthly return rate, n = number of months. Step‑up and inflation are applied iteratively each year.
20. Can I share my SIP calculation with a friend?
Yes – click the “Share” button to copy a URL containing all your inputs. Anyone opening that link will see the exact same SIP plan and results.
