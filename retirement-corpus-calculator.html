What Is a Retirement Corpus Calculator?

A retirement corpus calculator estimates the total amount of savings you need to accumulate by the time you retire, so that you can sustain your desired lifestyle for the rest of your life. It accounts for factors such as your current monthly expenses, expected inflation, life expectancy, and the rate of return on your investments (both before and after retirement). The retirement corpus calculation ensures you do not outlive your savings.

Our pension corpus calculator goes beyond basic formulas. It allows you to input existing savings, monthly contributions until retirement, expected inflation (e.g., 6%), pre‑retirement return rate (e.g., 10%), post‑retirement return rate (e.g., 5%), and desired retirement years. The tool then computes the required corpus and shows a year‑by‑year projection of your wealth accumulation and drawdown.

How to Use This Retirement Corpus Calculator

1 Enter your current monthly expenses – the amount you need for living costs today.
2 Set your current age and desired retirement age – how many years until retirement.
3 Enter life expectancy – the number of years you want your corpus to last in retirement.
4 Set expected inflation rate – e.g., 6% for India, 3% for US/EU.
5 Choose pre‑retirement return rate – expected annual return on your investments while saving (e.g., 10% for equity).
6 Choose post‑retirement return rate – conservative return after retirement (e.g., 5% for debt).
7 Add existing savings and monthly contributions – your current retirement fund and how much you save each month.
8 Click Calculate – view the required retirement corpus, how much you will accumulate, and a year‑by‑year projection.

Frequently Asked Questions About Retirement Corpus Calculators

1. What is a retirement corpus?
A retirement corpus is the total savings you need to accumulate by the time you retire so that you can generate a regular income to cover your expenses for the rest of your life.
2. How is retirement corpus calculated?
The formula considers your current expenses, expected inflation, number of years until retirement, life expectancy, and expected returns. It uses a retirement corpus formula that accounts for both accumulation and drawdown phases.
3. Why is inflation important in retirement planning?
Inflation reduces purchasing power. At 6% inflation, expenses double every 12 years. Our retirement corpus calculator with inflation ensures you don't underestimate future needs.
4. What is a realistic return assumption for retirement savings?
Pre‑retirement: 8-10% for equity‑heavy portfolios, 6-7% for balanced. Post‑retirement: 4-6% due to safer debt funds. Adjust based on your risk tolerance.
5. Can I use this calculator for NPS (National Pension System)?
Yes – an nps corpus calculator works the same way. Enter your NPS contributions and expected returns to estimate the final corpus.
6. How does life expectancy affect the corpus needed?
Longer life expectancy means more years in retirement, requiring a larger corpus. Plan for at least 90 years of age to be safe.
7. What is the rule of thumb for retirement corpus?
The 4% rule says your corpus should be 25× your first year’s expenses. For example, ₹10L/year expenses → ₹2.5Cr corpus. But this rule may not hold with high inflation.
8. Can I include my spouse's expenses and savings?
Yes – combine your expenses and savings. For a couple, consider that one may outlive the other, so plan for a longer retirement period.
9. Does the calculator work for any currency?
Yes – select your currency (INR, USD, EUR, GBP, PKR, etc.). All expenses and corpus will be displayed in that currency.
10. What if I don't have any existing savings?
Enter zero. The calculator will show the monthly savings required from today to reach your goal. It's never too late to start.
11. Can I see a year‑by‑year projection?
Yes – the tool provides a table showing your portfolio growth each year until retirement, and then how it depletes each year during retirement.
12. How do I adjust for part‑time work or pension income?
Reduce your monthly expense requirement by the expected pension or part‑time income. The calculator assumes you need to cover the gap.
13. What is the difference between a retirement corpus calculator and a pension corpus calculator?
They are essentially the same. “Pension corpus” often refers to the fund needed to generate a regular pension income. Our tool covers both.
14. Can I use this for early retirement (FIRE)?
Absolutely. For FIRE, set retirement age earlier (e.g., 40) and plan for a longer retirement (e.g., 50 years). Use a lower withdrawal rate (e.g., 3%).
15. How does the calculator handle market volatility?
It assumes a constant annual return – a simplification. For conservative planning, use lower return assumptions to account for volatility.
16. Can I download my retirement plan?
Yes – click “Download CSV” to save the year‑by‑year projection. You can also share your calculation via a unique URL.
17. What if I want to leave an inheritance?
Increase your target corpus. The calculator currently assumes you spend down to zero. For inheritance, aim for a corpus that grows even after withdrawals.
18. Is this calculator accurate for high inflation countries?
Yes – you can set inflation up to 20%. The calculator uses the same formula regardless of inflation rate.
19. Is the calculator free?
Yes – 100% free, no signup, no ads. Use it for personal or professional retirement planning.
20. Can I use this calculator for my parents' retirement?
Yes – enter their current age, expenses, and existing savings. The calculator will show the corpus needed and any shortfall to cover.
