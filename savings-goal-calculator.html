The Savings Goal Formula

The savings goal formula calculates how long it will take to reach your target based on your current savings, monthly contributions, and expected return:

FV = PV × (1 + r)n + PMT × ((1 + r)n − 1) / r Where: FV = future value (goal), PV = present value (current savings), r = monthly rate, n = number of months, PMT = monthly contribution

For example, if you have $5,000 saved, add $500/month, earn 8% annually, and want to reach $25,000 — you'll achieve your goal in approximately 34 months and earn $1,700 in interest.

Frequently Asked Questions About Savings Goals

1. What is a savings goal calculator?
A savings goal calculator is a tool that helps you plan how to reach a financial target. It calculates how long it will take to reach your goal based on your current savings, monthly contributions, and expected returns.
2. How do I calculate how much to save each month?
Use this financial goal calculator — enter your goal, current savings, and expected return. The calculator will show you the monthly contribution needed to reach your goal by your target date.
3. What is a good savings goal?
A good savings goal depends on your situation. Common goals include a 3‑6 month emergency fund, a down payment for a home (10‑20%), or a specific purchase like a car or vacation. Use this savings target calculator to plan your unique goal.
4. How does compound interest help me reach my savings goal?
Compound interest helps you reach your goal faster by earning interest on your interest. This savings goal calculator shows you exactly how much interest you'll earn over time.
5. Can I use this calculator for weekly contributions?
Yes. You can use this savings goal planner for any contribution frequency. To calculate a weekly contribution, simply divide your planned monthly contribution by 4 or enter a custom amount.
6. What should I do if I'm not on track to reach my savings goal?
If you're falling behind, consider increasing your monthly contribution, extending your timeline, or reducing your goal amount. Use this money goal calculator to adjust your plan and see different scenarios.
7. How accurate is this savings goal calculator?
This savings target calculator uses standard financial formulas and is accurate for planning. However, actual returns may vary — use it as a guide, not a guarantee.
