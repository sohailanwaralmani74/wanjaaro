What Is a FIRE Number Calculator?

A FIRE number calculator helps you determine the amount of wealth (corpus) you need to achieve financial independence and retire early (FIRE – Financial Independence, Retire Early). The core principle is the 4% rule: you can withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually without depleting it for 30+ years. Your FIRE number is simply your expected annual expenses multiplied by 25 (since 1 ÷ 0.04 = 25). For example, if you need ₹10,00,000 per year, your FIRE number is ₹2.5 crore.

Our financial independence number calculator goes beyond the basic rule. You can adjust the withdrawal rate (e.g., 3% for conservative planners), account for inflation, include existing savings, set a target retirement age, and even factor in future income streams like pension or rental income. The tool provides a year‑by‑year projection showing when you will reach your FIRE goal.

Frequently Asked Questions About FIRE Number Calculators

1. What is the FIRE number?
The FIRE number is the amount of wealth you need to accumulate to live off your investments indefinitely. It is typically calculated as annual expenses divided by the safe withdrawal rate (e.g., 25× expenses for 4% withdrawal).
2. What is the 4% rule?
The 4% rule states that you can withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually, adjusted for inflation, and the portfolio will last 30+ years. It’s the foundation for most FIRE calculations.
3. How do I calculate my FIRE number?
Multiply your expected annual expenses by 25 (for 4% withdrawal). For example, ₹10L/year expenses → ₹2.5Cr. Our calculator allows custom withdrawal rates and inflation adjustments.
4. What is a safe withdrawal rate (SWR) for early retirement?
For traditional retirement (30 years), 4% is safe. For early retirement (50+ years), many FIRE proponents use 3-3.5% to avoid running out of money.
5. Does inflation affect my FIRE number?
Yes – your expenses will rise with inflation. Our calculator projects both nominal and real corpus, so you see the future value needed.
6. How do I choose my expected return on investment?
For a diversified equity‑heavy portfolio (typical for FIRE), use 8-10% nominal return. For conservative portfolios, use 6-7%.
7. Can I include existing savings in the calculation?
Absolutely. Enter your current retirement or investment corpus. The calculator will show how much more you need to save and the remaining time to reach your FIRE number.
8. What if I want to retire earlier than the projection?
You can adjust your savings rate, increase investment returns (by taking more risk), or lower your expected expenses. The calculator lets you experiment with different scenarios.
9. Does the calculator account for taxes?
Not directly. For more accurate planning, reduce your expected return to post‑tax returns (e.g., 12% pre‑tax → ~10.2% after LTCG on equity).
10. What is the difference between FIRE number and retirement corpus?
FIRE number is specifically for early retirement with a focus on sustainable withdrawal. Retirement corpus is a broader term – but the math is essentially the same.
11. Can I use this calculator for a couple (shared expenses)?
Yes – enter combined annual expenses, combined current savings, and combined monthly contributions. The calculator works for any household.
12. What is the impact of a lower withdrawal rate (e.g., 3%)?
A 3% withdrawal rate means your FIRE number is 33× annual expenses (instead of 25×). This requires a larger corpus but provides a higher safety margin for very early retirement.
13. Does the calculator support different currencies?
Yes – select your currency (INR, USD, EUR, GBP, PKR, etc.). The FIRE number will be displayed in that currency with proper formatting.
14. How do I interpret the year‑by‑year chart?
The chart shows your growing corpus (with contributions and compounding) and a horizontal line representing your FIRE target. The year when the two lines cross is your estimated FIRE date.
15. Can I include future income (e.g., pension, rental) in the calculation?
Not directly in this tool. However, you can reduce your annual expense requirement by the expected passive income, then calculate the FIRE number on the reduced expense amount.
16. Is the 4% rule safe for Indian investors?
Some studies suggest a lower rate (3-3.5%) due to higher inflation and market volatility in India. Our calculator lets you set any withdrawal rate to test different scenarios.
17. Can I save or share my FIRE calculation?
Yes – use the “Share” button to copy a URL containing all your inputs. You can also download the year‑by‑year projection as CSV.
18. What is the maximum time horizon allowed?
Up to 50 years – sufficient for most FIRE plans (retiring at 40 gives 50 years of retirement).
19. Is this calculator free?
Yes – completely free, no signup, no ads. Use it as many times as you need to refine your financial independence plan.
20. Can I use this for Lean FIRE or Fat FIRE?
Absolutely. Just adjust your annual expenses: Lean FIRE uses minimal living expenses (e.g., ₹6L/year), Fat FIRE uses a more luxurious budget (e.g., ₹20L/year). The calculator works for any target.
