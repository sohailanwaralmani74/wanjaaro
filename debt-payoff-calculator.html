The Debt Payoff Formula

The debt payoff formula calculates how long it takes to eliminate your debt based on your monthly payment and interest rate:

n = −log(1 − (r × P) / M) / log(1 + r) Where: n = number of months, r = monthly interest rate, P = principal (total debt), M = monthly payment

For example, if you owe $10,000 at 18% interest and pay $500 per month, you'll be debt free in approximately 24 months — and you'll pay $1,988 in total interest.

What Is Amortization?

Amortization is the process of paying off debt through regular monthly payments. Each payment covers both principal (the amount you borrowed) and interest (the cost of borrowing).

Early in your debt repayment, a larger portion of each payment goes toward interest. Over time, as the principal decreases, more of your payment goes toward the principal. An amortization schedule shows this breakdown month by month — and this debt repayment calculator generates it instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Debt Payoff

1. How does making extra payments affect my debt payoff?
Making extra payments reduces your principal faster, which lowers the total interest you'll pay and can shorten your debt payoff timeline by months or even years. This debt payoff calculator shows you the impact of different payment amounts.
2. What is a good monthly payment for paying off debt?
A "good" monthly payment is one that exceeds your minimum payment and fits your budget. The higher your payment, the faster you'll become debt free and the less interest you'll pay. Use this pay down debt calculator to find the right balance for your situation.
3. What is an amortization schedule?
An amortization schedule is a complete breakdown of every monthly payment over the life of your debt. It shows how much of each payment goes toward principal and interest, and what your remaining balance is after each payment.
4. Should I pay off debt or save money first?
Generally, pay off high‑interest debt (above 10%) as quickly as possible. For low‑interest debt (below 5%), you might consider saving and investing while making regular payments. Use this debt repayment calculator to see the true cost of your debt.
5. How does the interest rate affect my debt payoff timeline?
A higher interest rate means more of your payment goes toward interest, which slows down your payoff and increases total interest. A lower rate accelerates your payoff and reduces total interest. This debt payoff estimator helps you see the difference.
6. Can I use this calculator for different types of debt?
Yes. This debt payment calculator works for any fixed‑rate debt — including credit cards, personal loans, student loans, car loans, and mortgages. Enter your balance, interest rate, and monthly payment to see your plan.
7. How accurate is this debt payoff calculator?
This debt payoff planner uses standard financial formulas and is accurate for planning. However, actual results may vary if interest rates change or if you miss payments. Always check with your lender for final numbers.
