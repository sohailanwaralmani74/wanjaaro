What Is a Future Value Calculator?

A future value calculator (FV calculator) estimates the worth of an investment or savings account at a specific date in the future, accounting for compound interest, periodic contributions, and inflation. It applies the core principle of the time value of money: money today is worth more than the same amount in the future because it can grow through investment. Our future worth calculator handles both lump sum and monthly/yearly contributions, multiple compounding frequencies, and inflation adjustment – giving you a complete year‑by‑year projection with charts.

Whether you are saving for retirement, a child’s education, or a large purchase, knowing the future value of your current savings and regular contributions helps you set realistic goals. Use this tool to answer questions like: “What will my ₹10,000 monthly SIP grow to in 15 years at 12% return?” or “How much will a one‑time ₹5 lakh investment become after 20 years with 8% compounding?”

Frequently Asked Questions About Future Value Calculators

1. What is future value (FV)?
Future value is the amount of money an investment will grow to at a future date, assuming a specific rate of return and compounding frequency. It is the core concept of the time value of money.
2. What is the future value formula?
For a lump sum: FV = PV × (1 + r/n)^(n×t) where PV = present value, r = annual rate, n = compounding periods per year, t = years. For periodic contributions, the formula sums each deposit’s compounded growth.
3. How is future value different from present value?
Present value (PV) discounts future money to today’s terms. Future value (FV) projects today’s money forward. Both are two sides of the time value of money coin.
4. Can I calculate future value with monthly contributions?
Yes – our calculator supports monthly and yearly contributions. Each contribution compounds from the date it is added, giving a more accurate projection for SIPs or recurring deposits.
5. What compounding frequency should I use?
Daily gives the highest future value, monthly is standard for most investments, and annual is simplest. The difference between daily and monthly is very small – typically less than 0.1% per year.
6. How does inflation affect future value?
Inflation reduces real purchasing power. Our inflation adjustment shows the future value in today’s money – i.e., the real value after accounting for price increases.
7. What is a good expected rate of return for future value calculations?
For equity mutual funds: 10-12% p.a. for long term. For debt funds: 6-8%. For conservative planning use 8% for equities and 5% for debt.
8. Can I calculate future value for a lump sum only?
Yes – set monthly and yearly contributions to zero. The calculator will project the growth of your initial investment alone.
9. Does the calculator work for any currency?
Yes – select your currency (INR, USD, EUR, GBP, PKR, etc.). The future value will be displayed with the correct symbol and formatting.
10. How do I interpret the year‑by‑year table?
The table shows for each year: balance (future value), total contributed, interest earned, and growth percentage. You can see how your money accumulates over time.
11. Can I use this for retirement planning?
Absolutely. Enter your current retirement savings as initial investment, add monthly contributions, choose a realistic return (e.g., 8-10%), and set the number of years until retirement. The result is your projected retirement corpus.
12. What is the difference between future value and maturity value?
They are often used interchangeably. Maturity value typically refers to fixed deposits or bonds at the end of term, while future value is a broader financial term.
13. Can I calculate future value for an annuity (regular payments)?
Yes – this is exactly what our monthly contribution feature does. It calculates the future value of an ordinary annuity (payments at start of period).
14. How accurate is the future value calculation?
It uses the standard compound interest formula with exact mathematics. However, actual future returns are uncertain – the calculator assumes a constant rate, which rarely happens in real markets. Use it for planning, not guarantees.
15. Can I save or share my future value calculation?
Yes – use the “Share” button to copy a URL that contains all your inputs. You can also download the year‑by‑year table as CSV.
16. What is the maximum investment period?
Up to 50 years. The slider and input cap at 50 years, but that covers most long‑term goals like retirement.
17. Does the calculator account for taxes?
No – it shows pre‑tax future value. To estimate post‑tax, reduce the expected return manually (e.g., 12% pre‑tax → ~10.2% after LTCG tax on equity).
18. What is the difference between future value and future worth?
They are synonyms. Both refer to the value of an asset or cash at a specified future date.
19. Can I use this calculator for education planning?
Yes – enter your current college savings, monthly contributions, expected return, and years until the child starts college. The future value tells you the projected education corpus.
