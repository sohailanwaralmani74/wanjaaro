What Is an EMI Calculator?

An EMI calculator (Equated Monthly Installment calculator) helps you determine your fixed monthly payment towards a loan – whether it's a home loan, personal loan, car loan, or business loan. The calculator uses the standard EMI formula based on the loan amount, annual interest rate, and loan tenure. Understanding your equated monthly installment is essential for budgeting and comparing loan offers from different lenders.

Our loan EMI calculator goes beyond the basic monthly payment. It provides a full amortisation schedule showing how much principal and interest you pay each month, the total interest payable over the loan term, and the impact of making prepayments. You can also calculate eligibility and compare different loan scenarios by adjusting the rate, tenure, or down payment.

How to Use This Loan EMI Calculator

1 Enter the loan amount – the principal you wish to borrow.

Frequently Asked Questions About EMI Calculators

1. What is EMI?
EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment – a fixed monthly payment that you make to repay a loan. It includes both principal repayment and interest.
2. How is EMI calculated?
The formula is: EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n – 1), where P = loan principal, r = monthly interest rate (annual rate/12), n = number of monthly installments.
3. What is an amortisation schedule?
An amortisation table shows each monthly payment broken down into principal and interest, along with the remaining loan balance after each payment.
4. Can I use this calculator for a home loan?
Yes – our home loan EMI calculator works perfectly. Enter your loan amount, interest rate, and tenure (typically 15-30 years) to see your monthly EMI.
5. Can I use this for a personal loan?
Absolutely – our personal loan EMI calculator works for any unsecured loan. Personal loan tenures are typically 1-5 years, and interest rates range from 10-18%.
6. Does the calculator work for car loans?
Yes – our car loan EMI calculator and auto emi calculator are ready. Enter your vehicle loan amount, interest rate (typically 8-12%), and tenure (3-7 years).
7. How does loan tenure affect EMI?
Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest paid. Shorter tenure increases EMI but saves interest. Use the calculator to compare different tenures.
8. What is the impact of a higher interest rate?
Even a 0.5% increase can raise your EMI significantly over long tenures. The calculator shows the difference instantly.
9. Can I make prepayments on my loan?
Yes – our prepayment feature lets you enter an extra amount. The calculator recalculates either reduced tenure or reduced EMI, showing interest savings.
10. What is the difference between EMI and simple interest?
EMI includes reducing balance interest – interest is calculated on the outstanding principal each month. Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal.
11. Can I calculate EMI for a business loan?
Yes – our business loan EMI calculator works for any commercial loan. Enter the loan amount, interest rate (typically 10-15%), and tenure (1-7 years).
12. Can I use this for an education loan?
Absolutely – our education loan EMI calculator helps students and parents plan repayments. Education loans often have moratorium periods (no payment during study).
13. How do I choose the right EMI for my budget?
Financial experts recommend that EMI should not exceed 30-40% of your monthly income. Use the calculator to find a loan amount and tenure that fits your budget.
14. Does the calculator support different currencies?
Yes – select your currency (USD, EUR, GBP, INR, PKR, etc.). The EMI and loan amounts will display in your chosen currency with proper formatting.
