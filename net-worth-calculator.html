What Is Net Worth and Why Does It Matter?

Your net worth is the total value of everything you own (assets) minus everything you owe (liabilities). It's the clearest picture of your financial health — a snapshot of where you stand today.

The Net Worth Formula

The net worth formula is simple:

Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities

For example, if your assets total $365,000 and your liabilities total $208,000, your net worth is $157,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Net Worth

1. What is net worth?
Net worth is the total value of everything you own (assets) minus everything you owe (liabilities). It's a snapshot of your financial health.
2. How do I calculate my net worth?
Use the net worth formula: Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities. Or simply use this net worth calculator — it does the math for you instantly.
3. What is a good net worth?
A "good" net worth depends on your age, income, and goals. Generally, a positive net worth that grows over time is the goal. Use this personal net worth calculator to track your progress.
4. What assets should I include in my net worth?
Include all major assets: savings, home value, investments, retirement funds, car value, and other property. This net worth tracker helps you list and value everything.
5. Should I include my mortgage in my net worth?
Yes. Your mortgage is a liability. Subtract it from your assets when calculating your net worth. The net worth formula includes all debts.
6. How often should I calculate my net worth?
Review your net worth monthly or quarterly. Regular tracking helps you stay motivated and adjust your financial plan. This net worth estimator makes it easy to update your numbers.
